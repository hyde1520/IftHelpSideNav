National HVRA: Infrastructure - Surface Drinking Water

Brief summary

This dataset includes coverage for the conterminous US.

Description

Overview

Surface Drinking Water is part of the Highly Valued Resources or Assets (HVRA) data used in the National Wildfire Risk Assessment for Forest Service Lands (NaWRA, Dillon 2020). Surface Drinking Water can be used as sub-HVRAs within the Map Values, Exposure Analysis and Quantitative Wildfire Risk Assessment functionality within the Interagency Fuel Treatment Decision Support System (, https://iftdss.firenet.gov)

Data Details

The Surface Drinking Water was derived from the USDA Forest Service Forests to Faucets project (Weidner and Todd 2012, https://www.fs.fed.us/ecosystemservices/FS_Efforts/forests2faucets.shtml ). The Surface Drinking Water Importance (IMP) index takes into account water supply, spatial flow through the landscape, and the downstream drinking water demand (Weidner and Todd 2012). The IMP index was mapped to the hydrologic unit 12 (HUC 12 scale). Non-zero IMP index values were split into 100 quantiles, so the values range from 0 to 100.

Surface Drinking Water for each HUC 12 was assigned to one of ten classes based on the IMP index value:

  • Lowest importance (IMP 1-10)
  • Extremely low importance (IMP 11-20)
  • Very low importance (IMP 21-30)
  • Low importance (IMP 31-40)
  • Moderately low importance (IMP 41-50)
  • Moderately high importance (IMP 51-60)
  • High importance (IMP 61-70)
  • Very high importance (IMP 71-80)
  • Extremely high importance (IMP 81-90)
  • Highest importance (IMP 91-100)
