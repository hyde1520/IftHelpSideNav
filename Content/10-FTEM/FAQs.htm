Wildfires

Missing or Incorrect Wildfires? Why Is my Wildfire Represented as a Point?

Wildfire point data is pulled nightly from the Integrated Reporting of Wildland Fire Information (IRWIN) system. If no perimeter has been loaded into NIFS by the GISS via the Hosted National Incident Feature service for your wildfire, you may only see an origin point or triangular polygon representing the location, regardless of the acreage.

  If you have a wildfire location and would like to analyze polygon data from your local machine when monitoring (i.e. if no perimeter is available), please see Adding Shapefiles in FTEM.

For more detail see FTEM Wildfire Technical Documentation.

 What if my Wildfire Perimeter Doesn't Show Up Correctly?

To display correctly, the wildfire perimeter must be correctly entered into the system of record (NIFS), this includes having the correct IRWIN ID for the perimeter.

Check your agency's system of record. Contact dispatch for local fires and the GISS or SITL for larger incidents and request the fire perimeter be added or corrected in the system of record.

 Does my Region Affect What Fires I Monitor?

No. The region you specify when you create your FTEM account simply constrains the map extent you see when you first open FTEM. You are still free to scroll and search for fires/treatments anywhere on the FTEM map.

The list of fires and interactions you see in FTEM is determined by the map area you are zoomed into. It includes all agency fires so that fires which burn onto and through your agency lands are accounted for.

Why do FTEM and FIRESTAT differ?

FTEM gets fire data through IRWIN, which gathers fire data from FireCode, WildCAD, Sit/209, and WFDSS. Currently FIRESTAT, the final fire reporting system for the USFS, is "read-only" for IRWIN data, which means that changes made in FIRESTAT do not propagate back into IRWIN. Thus, fire data in FTEM may not reflect the same values seen in the FIRESTAT application. Efforts are underway to have Forest Service final fire reports reflected in IRWIN within the next year or two.

Treatments

How do I filter a long list of treatments?

You may filter a long list of treatments or find out more treatment information using the treatment list of the map. See the Filtering and Viewing Treatment Information in FTEM topic to see the steps for each method.

 What if a treatment is missing or incorrect? What if not all the treatment listed for a fire need monitoring?

Treatments that did not interact with a fire can be removed from that fire by following the steps on the Remove Treatments topic.

 What if treatments are loaded into FTEM after I've started monitoring a wildfire?

The new treatments will be available once in FTEM for users to add manually but the auto detect system will not pick them up once a fire has had monitoring started on it. So if you are missing treatments or think they have been updated in the system, you must select them on the map and add them from there or choose the add treatments button in the treatments tab to add them.

 How is the treatments list automatically populated once I select a wildfire?

Treatments are listed for each based on intersection with the fire perimeter or proximity to a fire point.

For more detail you may consult the FTEM Technical Documentation on buffering, treatments, and wildfires.

Treatment Interactions

My wildfire didn't interact with treatments but FTEM says it did, how do I correct it?

Treatments that did not interact with a fire can be removed from that fire by following the steps on the Remove Treatments topic.

 How do I monitor a fire that interacts with treatments from multiple agencies?

For FTEM monitoring, each agency will enter data on their own treatments.

If you find it helpful, you may view different agency treatment layers using the Add layers widget when in the map view.

For those generating FTEM reports, the reports for all agencies will be available once the monitoring is completed.

If you need an FTEM role for more than one agency:

Request access to more than agency role from your FTEM User Profile. Each role must be approved by a FTEM Agency Lead. To see the full details on how to do this, see the Updating your User Role section of FTEM Accounts.

 If I complete monitoring and later need to update or edit, can I do that? If so, how?

A wildfire where FTEM has been completed can be updated or edited. Once you locate your fire in FTEM, go to the Complete tab and select Amend. Provide a simple justification for the change into the pop-up box and you will be able to make any necessary adjustments.

 Why is the "Treatment acreage burned by wildfire" field blank for my interaction polygon?

If a treatment point treatment is monitored or partially monitored, then later gets updated to a polygon, the "Treatment acreage burned by wildfire" field will be blank. If this occurs, you may: 

  • Input the treatment interactions manually
  • Make a note of the existing monitoring data, then remove the treatment from the wildfire, and re-add the treatment to the fire. This will remove the monitoring data for the treatment, but will auto-populate the interaction acres. The monitoring data will have to be re-populated.

Account Management

I am changing jobs, what do I do with my FTEM account?
  • If you are responsible for FTEM in your new position and have never had access before, simply request FTEM access once you have updated your profile email address.
  • If you were responsible for FTEM in a previous position but have changed geographic locations within your agency, your preferred region will continue to reflect your previous region. This will not impact your work in any way.
  • If you were responsible for FTEM (Agency Editor or Higher) in a previous position with another agency, you have two options:

    • Request a new account with your new email address and request FTEM access with this new agency account.

      If you pursue this option you will not have access to any work done with your previous account from this new account so will be maintaining two accounts with different email addresses.

    • Change your email address in your existing account and contact the FTEM Agency Administrator for your new agency. They can set your new roles and privileges. You will not have a region set with your new account but it doesn’t matter.
 When creating an FTEM Account, what is the significance of providing my region/unit?

Specifying your region or unit when creating an FTEM account does not affect your access to wildfire data.

FTEM uses your region/unit in two ways:

  • The region is used to determine which region of the United States to display in map view when you open an FTEM monitoring session. You may still zoom in, zoom out, or scroll to any other region of the map.

  • The region determines who your FTEM Administrator is. If you request changes in your FTEM role, an FTEM Administrator for your region will be notified to review the request. Support desk agents may also view the region listed on your account if they need to find an alternate FTEM Administrator to review your request (for example if your usual FTEM Administrator is away on assignment or otherwise unavailable).

General

Is FTEM Data Available as a Geodatabase (.gdb)?

FTEM Agency Administrators have access to FTEM data in .gdb format. If you have and Editor role and would like access to data in .gdb format, please contact your FTEM Agency Administrator to request a copy.

 Can I upload a shapefile if my wildfire is just represented in FTEM by a point?

Yes, temporarily. You can upload shapes for viewing purposes to help you complete your monitoring, the shapes are not retained when you leave the system.

To permanently add a shapefile for a fire or treatment you'll need to update the systems of record FTEM uses (FACTS or NFPORS for treatments, IRWIN or NIFS for wildfires).

 A treatment for 2018 has already been entered into the legacy FTEM system, do I need to enter it again here?

No. You do not need to re-enter 2018 data into the FTEM new version of , if you have already entered it in the legacy system.

 Is data that had been loaded into FTEM in the years before 2017 included in the new system?

Data prior to 2017 is not loaded into the current version of FTEM. If you need to view that data you will have to access the legacy FTEM system and contact the agency lead for FTEM.

 What file types can I upload into FTEM as attachments?

Using the Add Attachments button, users can upload MS Word documents, PDFs, JPGs, GIFs, and PNGs.

If you have a video file, that is stored in a stable location, you may include the link in a .Doc or PDF file.

 Is there a deadline for FTEM Monitoring?

Individual policy for each agency is available in FTEM Policy Guidance.

 Local Times? I understand the wildfire data comes in from IRWIN. The start times for my wildfires in AZ seem to be in Greenwich Mean Time and are 7 hours ahead. The Control, Contain, and Out times are all 5 hours ahead. Will that be changed to reflect local times?
