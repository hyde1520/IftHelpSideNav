FTEM Monitoring Tutorial

This tutorial covers the steps of:

  • Selecting a Wildfire

  • Selecting Treatments

  • Monitoring Treatments

  • Complete Monitoring

Step 1: Select a Wildfire

Select a wildfire to monitor by searching for the name, narrowing by year or agency, or other filtering options:

  3. In addition to the search field, you may also filter the fire list to locate and view additional fire information, or filter in the map view.

  4. This auto-populates the next tab, Treatments, with a list of all treatments that overlapped selected fire. See the Buffering Rules documentation for more information on how treatments are auto-populated.

    5. Need to de-select your fire? Move the mouse cursor to the Wildfire tab and click the black X to the left of the wildfire name.

  5. (Optional) Click the Zoom To button to zoom to the fire on the map, or the Table Summary button to see a summary of the interactions for that fire.

  6. Proceed to the Treatments tab by clicking the tab. You may also proceed to Treatments by clicking theTreatments button located under the wildfire name you've selected.

Attached files can be viewed, added, or downloaded at anytime if needed (see Attachments). Notice that each wildfire listed is followed by information, left to right, describing the number of acres, control date, monitor status, and the number of interactions.

Need to find a fire discovery date? That information can be found using the Identify widget, or moving the fire list to show up full screen to show the right hand panel metadata.

Step 2: Select Treatments

In this step you choose all the treatments for your agency that you know interacted or influenced the fire to form a list of interacting treatments. These treatments are monitored in the next tab (Monitoring). You can think of this treatment selection step as the "shopping cart" where you place the treatments that require monitoring, and remove the ones that don't.

The Treatment list for a fire is shared by all agency personnel monitoring the fire, you may need to collaborate with monitors from adjacent units to make sure you do not remove treatments others will need to monitor.

  4. Note the colored icons to the left of the treatment names. In this example the polygon icons indicate these treatments are polygons, and their red color indicates monitoring/data entry for them has not been started. Learn more about these symbols by visiting FTEM Symbology.

  5. Proceed to Monitor by clicking the Monitor tab, or by clicking one of the Monitor Treatment(s) buttons at the top or bottom of the treatment list.

Notice that each treatment listed is followed by information describing the treatment ID, type of treatment, date of completion, number of acres for the entire treatment, and agency.

Step 4: Complete Monitoring

In the Complete tab:

  1. A list of "completed", "in progress", and "not started" treatments is displayed at the bottom of the page for your agency. If multiple agencies are involved, this is displayed for each agency.

  2.  

When the Complete Wildfire Monitoring button is grayed out, there are still treatments unmonitored for your agency. To make the button active, you either need to finish monitoring treatments for your agency in the monitoring tab, remove treatments that did not interact with the fire in the Treatments tab, or collaborate with personnel in your agency on adjacent units who still need to monitor their treatments.

FTEM Monitoring Tutorial

The FTEM interface walks you through the treatment monitoring process. Use the tabs to select a wildfire, select your treatments, enter monitoring data, and review and complete your monitoring. Below, we walk through each stage of this process. The following steps assume you have an FTEM account. If not, visit the FTEM Setup topic for instructions. Upon opening FTEM, you will see wildfires with a split view of Map Studio on the left, and a table view on the right.

FTEM Interface Controls

Each button and tab in the FTEM interface is labeled below:

By default, the interface is split, displaying a map on the left, and monitoring tabs on the right. Change the interface at anytime to show only the tabs or only the map by clicking the split screen buttons in the top of the screen just below the top navigation menu. Splitting the screen to only focus on the tabs also reveals a right hand panel displaying metadata, as shown below:

The default map layers visible include:

  • Wildfire Points: Described in more detail in Wildfire technical documentation.
  • Wildfire Polygons: Described in more detail in the Treatments and Buffering technical documentation.
  • Jurisdictional Agencies Reference Layer
  • LANDFIRE 2014 Fuel Models: The 40 fuels models is visible by default, 13 is also available.
