Wildfire List Tips

  • By default, the wildfire list in FTEM is sorted by number of fires. Clicking on a heading title, such as "Acres" or "Control Date" reorders the wildfire list according to that feature. If "Control Date" is null for a fire "Out Date" will be used, or "Contain Date" if "Out Date" is null. If all three features and null, the column will be blank.

Where Fire Data Comes From and How Often it Updates

From our wildfire data page

Source

Wildfire data is pulled into the FTEM from: 

  • The National Incident Feature Services (NIFS) . This service provides fire perimeter (polygon) data, where it exists.
  • The Integrated Reporting of Wildland Fire Information (IRWIN) data exchange service. This service provides point locations and fire-related data for fires across the United States.

Timing

Wildfire data is pulled into FTEM nightly.

Incorrect Fire Data

From new incorrect fire page

Incorrect fires in FTEM are usually caused by error in the data sources FTEM pulls from.

Fires cannot be corrected in FTEM but must be corrected in the systems FTEM pulls from, either IRWIN or NIFS.

To correct a fire, contact your GIS Specialist to update the information in IRWIN or NIFS. The correction will then be pulled into FTEM with 24 hours of the update.
