Survey Results 2019-2020

Thanks to all who took the time to complete our user survey throughout 2019-2020. We've summarized the results from the responses below:

Who Uses ?

Forest Service and Bureau of Land Management Fuels Specialists, Fuels Techs, and Fire Management Officers (FMOs) who are intermediate to advanced users of fire models and geographic information systems (GIS), and have 16+ years of experience (61%), 11-15 years (23%), or 10 or less years (17%).

What Do Users Want to See Change?

Of the features listed in the survey, the top three users would like to see added to include smoke, enhanced mapping functionality, and prescribed burn planning support to compare weather scenarios, fuels, etc.

Comments left by users reenforce this while also expressing need for a shared login function for project collaboration.

Users comments indicating the need to add fire effects and smoke modeling
  • I would like to see a smoke modeling module. Both modeling smoke direction (i.e. VSMOKE) and emission/consumption model (i.e. CONSUME).
  • It would be very beneficial to enhance a Fire Effects Monitoring module, where users could upload plot photos and short write-ups of Fire Effects to share/present.
  • Items related to smoke management would depend on models used, inputs, among other things. We currently use V-SmokeGIS and VSmokeWEB for burn plans in addition to basic geometric screening - we then use Hysplit trajectory and concentration for burn day modeling. Merging smoke management virtual playground with would have value. They use similar data and are designed for similar scales.
  • CONSUME/FOFEM was a part of first version of and I would like to see it come back.
  • ... I would use [] on an almost daily basis if these things were added.
 User comments indicating the need for prescribed burn planning templates and capabilities
  • I cannot say for sure but it seemed like used to be more useful for burn plan writing several years ago. I had not tried to utilize since then and when I went to use it this fall to help write a burn plan many of the features were not there. This could be my own ignorance of how to utilize in its current design state.
  • I would use prescribed fire planning if that feature is ever turned on.
  • ...Looking forward to the RX fire functionality.
  • I believe is a VERY useful tool, specifically in project planning (NEPA). I've been lightly using since version 1. I know that I do not use the full capacity has available for modeling. It would be nice to have the Prescribed Fire Plan flow back. I really liked that it was it's own separate model in version 1 and would walk you through each step, inclusive of modeling, for completing the Interagency Form.
 User comments requesting finer resolution modeling
  • We would like to be able to use the summary reports in our education and outreach efforts with stakeholders and agencies, but have found the resolution of some of the map products in those reports is not at a scale that enables us to do that. The maps are at a zoomed out extent and often the area of interest is too small to decipher data within the particular map layer. We have communicated this to the Help desk and they said that is something that is in the works for the Development Team. That would be a really useful product for us!
  • For accuracy to improve it would be nice if modeling is possible at a smaller scale. LANDFIRE seems like it applies best at larger scales not always appropriate for individual project treatment areas (<100 acres).
