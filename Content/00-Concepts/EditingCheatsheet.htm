Getting Started: Landscape Editing

Introduction

users often edit landscapes to better represent the conditions on their home unit, or to simulate landscape conditions after fuel treatments. Editing can be performed across the entire landscape, or limited to an area of interest using a shapefile you've uploaded, or a shape you've created in , as a mask. When you edit a landscape, retains your original landscape, and created a new copy containing your edits, this means your original landscape is always retained.

There are two editing options in :

  • Default Fuels Treatment/Disturbance Rules: Pre-existing rules built into .
  • Add Landscape Edit Rules: Custom rules you create.

How to Make (some) Edits

Landscape editing often begins by selecting Landscape Edit in the Landscape Evaluation stage of the Planning Cycle.

The next steps are taken from the Editing Landscape Data topic, please refer to it for full details, or to see how user-defined rules are created. We will use Default Fuels Treatment/Disturbance Rules for this short example.

When clicking on landscape files that have been edited in My Workspace, you will see an option to View Edit Rules. This displays the edits that were made on that landscape.
