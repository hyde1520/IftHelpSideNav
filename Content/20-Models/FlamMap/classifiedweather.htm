The Condition (Select a Classified Weather Stream) option in will condition dead fuel moisture based on set scenarios.

In this version (3.1.1) of the only option is “Extreme,” which represents near-maximum or worst case conditions. Additional options will be available in future versions.

Checking a Pyrome for 'completion' with in :

For those wanting to better understand the source of the classified conditioning data within , a reference layer within Map Studio allows you to view a map of the pyromes within the Continental US and Alaska. As shown in the map of pryrome earlier, some areas do not have pryome data. To view this layer in , navigate to the approximate area in which you are working and follow these steps:

  1. Open the Add Layers widget.

  2. From the IFTDSS Reference Layers tab, scroll down and expand the “Other Layers” section and click the box next to “Pyrome”.

  3. You will notice several light gray boundary lines appear on the map. To view these better, open the Basemap Gallery and select the "Dark Gray Canvas" option. Close the basemap gallery.

  4. Open the Layer List widget, expand the “pryome” layer and view more options by left-clicking the More Options button to the right of the layer name.

  5. Scroll down and select Open Attribute Table. The attribute table will open at the bottom of the map.

  6. With the attribute table open, you can then find the row in the table representing your area by scrolling and viewing the row names. Highlight the area for a row by single left-clicking in the gray space to the far left of that row. Keep in mind that the table is only populated by what is in your visible map extent.

  7. Once you find your area, highlight the row and look at the “ExtremeWXS” column. If default conditioning information is available for that area, the field will say “Completed”. A blank field for the row indicates the area has not default classified conditioning data available.
