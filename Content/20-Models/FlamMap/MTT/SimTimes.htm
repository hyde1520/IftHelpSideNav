Simulation Time

Simulation time, or Burn Period Length, in hours, specifies the duration of the fire growth for each ignition during a Landscape Fire Behavior run. It assumes constant weather and is used to model individual burn periods within a wildfire rather than continuous spread of a wildfire over many days and weather scenarios.For each fire, fuel moisture and wind conditions you enter in the other input fields are held constant. Longer burn period lengths result in larger fires given all other conditions are unchanged.

Because inputs should represent the “worst case scenario” or “problem fire” conditions the duration of the burn period should reflect the active buring period of wildfires for your area under extreme conditions.

In IFTDSS, the Burn Period Length selected can be 4 to 12 hours long.
