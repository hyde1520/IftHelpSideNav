Burn Probability Inputs

Before running Landscape Burn Probability, note that modeling may take several hours to complete.

User inputs required to run Landscape Burn Probability (LBP) are outlined in the table below. For more detailed information, click on the links within the table.

Inputs Units Brief Description
  Wind
Wind Type Unitless Choose between gridded, static, uphill or downhill. See the Wind Options for more details.
20-ft Wind Speed Miles per hour Wind velocity measured 20 feet (6 m) above the top of the tree or shrub canopy, or above the ground if canopy is absent.
Wind Direction Degrees Wind direction is the direction from which the wind is blowing, specified in degrees increasing clockwise from north (which is zero degrees).
Crown Fire Inputs
Crown Fire Method Unitless Select one of two methods of calculating crown fire potential. See the Crown Fire Calculation Options Topic for more details.
Foliar Moisture Content Percentage Moisture content of the crown foliage. The mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle's oven-dry mass. See Initial and Foliar Moisture for more details.
  Initial Fuel Moisture
1-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 1-hour fuel (dead woody fuel 0 to ¼ inch or 0 to 6.4 mm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. See Initial and Foliar Moisture for more details.
10-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 10-hour fuel (dead woody fuel ¼ to 1 inch or 0.6 to 2.5 cm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. See Initial and Foliar Moisture for more details.
100-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 100-hour fuel (dead woody fuel 1 to 3 inches or 2.5 to 7.6 cm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. See Initial and Foliar Moisture for more details.
Live Herbaceous Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of live herbaceous fuel is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. See Initial and Foliar Moisture for more details.
Live Woody Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of live woody fuel is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. See Initial and Foliar Moisture for more details.
Fuel Moisture Conditioning
Conditioning Type Unitless Choose between initial, classified, or uploaded. See the Fuel Moisture Conditioning topic for more details.
Simulation Time
Burn Period Length Hours The duration of fire growth for each fire simulated. See the Simulation Time topic for more details.
Spotting
Spotting Probability Percentage The percentage of cells that launch embers when crown fire (passive or active) is initiated. See the Spotting Details topic for more details.

 

In addition to the inputs outlined in the table above, there are two inputs that are set by (Users cannot adjust these):

1) The resolution at which the model is run (which determines the resolution of the outputs). See the Run Resolution topic for more details.

2) The number of random ignitions modeled. See the Number of Fires Simulated topic for more details.

These automations exist to optimize model performance in generating the best modeled outputs possible based the time while accounting for the computation intensive nature of this model.
